Copacabana: A Town with History and Culture

Copacabana is a town on the shores of Lake Titicaca, with a rich pre-Hispanic history. Inhabited by Aymara communities, it is known for its imposing Copacabana Cathedral and its vibrant Artisan Market. Its cool and sunny climate makes it an ideal destination to visit at any time of the year, offering an unmissable combination of history, culture, and natural beauty.

Main Attractions in Copacabana:

  • Copacabana Cathedral: A beautiful colonial church dating from the 16th century and one of the main tourist attractions in the region.
  • Artisan Market: A place where you can find local handicrafts and souvenirs, such as textiles, ceramics, and jewelry.
  • Copacabana Beach: A quiet beach with views of Lake Titicaca, ideal for relaxing and enjoying the sun and water.
  • Horca del Inca: A historical place where the Incas executed prisoners, offering an impressive view of Lake Titicaca.
  • Copacabana Pier: An emblematic place where you can enjoy lake views and take a boat to the Island of the Sun or other nearby destinations.

Island of the Sun: A Sacred and Mysterious Place

The Island of the Sun is a tourist destination located on Lake Titicaca, with a rich history and culture. Divided into South Zone and North Zone, the island is known for its Inca ruins, such as the Temple of the Sun and the Inca Stairway. The cool and sunny climate, along with the natural beauty of the lake, makes it an ideal place to visit. The island is inhabited by Aymara communities and is a popular tourist destination due to its history and culture.

South Zone:

  • Temple of the Sun or Pilkokaina: A temple of Tiwanacota origin dedicated to the Sun God, with impressive views of the lake and the Island of the Moon.
  • Inca Stairway: A stone staircase that connects the port with the upper part of Yumani, where the inhabitants live and most accommodations are located.
  • Fountain of the Three Waters or of Eternal Youth: A fountain with three streams believed to have properties of wisdom, health, and youth.
  • Yumani Village: The most developed community on the island, with hotels, restaurants, and shops.

North Zone:

  • Chinkana Ruins or Labyrinth: An Inca archaeological complex with rooms and passages believed to have been used as warehouses or dwellings for priests.
  • The Ritual Table: A ceremonial altar where priests offered sacrifices to the Sun god, with impressive views of the lake.
  • The Sacred Rock: A sacred place for the Incas, where Manco Capac and Mama Ocllo, the founders of the Inca Empire, are believed to have emerged.
  • North Zone Beaches: Beaches with crystal clear and cold waters, ideal for relaxing and enjoying the natural environment.
  • Challapampa Village: The community living in the north of the island, with a quieter and more mystical atmosphere than the south zone.

Travel Times

  • La Paz to Copacabana: 3-4 hours by bus
  • Copacabana to the Island of the Sun (Tourist boat): 1-2 hours
    • Copacabana to South Zone Island of the Sun: 1-2 hours by boat
    • Copacabana to North Zone Island of the Sun: 1.5-2.5 hours by boat (depending on the type of boat and the route)
